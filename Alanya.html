About Alanya

A Rich Tapestry of Historyand Beauty

Discover the captivating city of Alanya, nestled on the scenic Turkish Riviera. With a history spanning thousands of years, this enchanting coastal town has witnessed the rise and fall of empires, leaving behind a rich tapestry of cultural heritage. Situated in the Antalya Province of Turkey, Alanya boasts a strategic location that has attracted settlers since ancient times. The city's roots can be traced back to the 4th century BC when it was known as Coracesium and served as akey port for trade between the Eastern Mediterranean and Anatolia. Throughout history, Alanya has been a coveted prize for numerous civilizations, including the Romans, Byzantines, Seljuks, and Ottomans. These diverse influences have left their mark on the city's architecture, traditions, and culture, creating a unique blend of East and West. One of Alanya's most iconic landmarks is the majestic Alanya Castle,perched high on a rocky peninsula overlooking the azure Mediterranean Sea.

Originally built by the Romans, the castle was further fortified by the Seljuks and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the coastline. Within the castle walls, visitors canexplore ancient ruins, includinga Byzantine church and a splendid Seljuk-era mosque. Alanya's historical significance extends beyond its castle. The Red Tower,a striking octagonal structure built by the Seljuks inthe 13th century, stands as a symbol of the city. It servedas a watchtower to defend the harbor and now houses a museum that provides insight into Alanya'spast. Today, Alanya continues to thrive as a popular tourist destination and a haven for real estate investment. With a thriving economy, modern infrastructure, anda favorable climate, it attracts visitors and expatriates from around the world seeking a vibrant coastal lifestyle.

The city center's vibrant streets are lined with traditional Ottoman houses,narrow alleys, and bustling bazaars. Explore the Alanya Shipyard, a remarkable medieval dry dock that once housed the Seljuk fleet. Wander through the lively Alanya Market, where locals and visitors alike gather to sample aromatic spices, handmade textiles, and fresh produce. Alanya's historical charm is complemented by its natural beauty. The city boasts miles of pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and picturesque cliffs.

Cleopatra Beach, named after the Egyptian queen who is said to have swum here, offers aparadise-like setting for sunbathing and water sports.
